SEO rule — just one
Before recording, type the title into YouTube's search bar
If it autocompletes, there's real demand. If the top results have under 100K views, you can compete. The title IS the search — if nobody would type it into YouTube, the title is wrong.
The system: Monday batch recording
🎬 Recording happens ONE day only: Monday. Voice and camera for all 3 videos of the week.
Recording is what takes the most energy and setup (lights, mic, being "camera-ready"). By concentrating it into one day, the setup happens once and the other 6 days are free for stress-free editing and publishing. It's the same system big channels use: batch recording.
🔍 Pre-production

Why batching works
A single "camera day" per week removes the biggest friction in content creation: getting ready to record. The rest of the week is calm editing work you can do at any hour. And if a Monday is ever missed, the previous week already left material as a buffer. Everything runs on templates — script, edit project, thumbnail — nothing is ever created from scratch twice.
The #1 metric: consistency
The only goal that matters: never miss a Monday
Views, subscribers and rankings are outcomes — you can't control them directly. What you CAN control is showing up: 1 recording day + 3 published videos, every single week. If the streak stays alive, everything else follows. The channel isn't measured in views; it's measured in weeks completed.

The 3 quality checks (after consistency)
👁️ CTR — Target: 5%+
Out of every 100 people who see the thumbnail, 5 click. If it's under 3%, change the thumbnail.
⏱️ Retention — Target: 45%+
On a 10-minute video, people watch at least 4.5 minutes. If they leave early, improve the hook.
➕ Sub rate — Target: 2%
2 subscribers per 100 views. The signal that the niche is well chosen.

How to use this bank
Every Friday, pick the next 3 (one from each of the top pillars when possible), validate each title in YouTube search, and write the scripts over the weekend. When the bank runs low, refill it: SteamDB for hidden gems, your own backlog for retrospectives, and YouTube autocomplete for "still worth playing" candidates. Never start a week without 3 validated topics.
